Welcome!

Ten One Tea House on Thayer Street has recently added a Snackpass kiosk for the convenience of its customers. Through this kiosk, they can place orders and pay without having to go to the cashier.

Introduction of Kiosk

Key Components of Kiosk Interface

  • Touchscreen ordering
  • Customization of order
  • Scrolling feature to see all menu items/menu categories
  • Able to pay using debit/credit
  • Adding a phone number to receive a text when order is ready


Observations and Interviews

We took the time to observe numerous customers and asked a few to share their experiences with the kiosk.

Key Observations

  • User experiences ranged from 1 - 6 minutes
  • The arrow button to return to the menu was frequently used
  • Some users spent longer during payment process
  • Users also made use of the "search" bar to find a specific order with a keyword
  • Users moved their fingers up to scroll down (replication of the mental model of pushing paper for scrolling)

Interview Questions

  1. How easy was it for you to navigate through the screens (very easy, somewhat easy, difficult, or very difficult)?
  2. What made you choose to order using the kiosk instead of ordering at the cashier?
  3. What did you like about using the kiosk?
  4. What did you dislike about using the kiosk?
  5. Would you recommend using the kiosk for other customers?

Summarized Responses

  • Interviewed users felt there was a shorter wait time to use the kiosk than ordering at the cashier.
  • Interviewed users felt the kiosk was either "very easy" or "somewhat easy" to use.
  • Interviewed users liked to see all the customization (add-ons, ice level, sugar level, etc.) on a screen rather than trying to remember.
  • Interviewed users felt there should have been a clearer notion to show where the card should be inserted to pay.
  • Interviewed users found it difficult to read the description or some of the text for the menu items.

Hypothetical Personas

For further exploration of the functionality of the kiosk, we have two crafted personas of potential Ten One Tea House customers that we will discuss using empathy maps and story boards.

Regular Rachel

As a junior at Brown, Regular Rachel is often visiting Thayer Street’s TenOne for her boba boost. Typically getting three bobas a week, she follows her routine of using the ordering kiosk before heading to the seating area to wait for her name to be called.

Confused Carson

As a first-year at Brown, Confused Carson is excited to try boba on Thayer Street. Seeing a store he’s not familiar with, he enters, overwhelmed by the long lines and people. Feeling assured by its popularity, he takes a quick peek at the menu on the wall before it's his turn to order at the kiosk. Never using the Snackpass kiosk, he is confused and feels pressure to hurry by the line behind him.

Learnings and Findings

Based on these observations and interviews, the Ten One Tea House kiosk has been effective and useful to consumers.

Benefits of the Kiosk

  • Users appreciate quickness and convenience.
  • The touchscreen interface with photos was helpful for decision-making.
  • Being able to customize orders was appreciated by customers.

Room for Improvement

  • Users should have direct instructions or arrows to indicate the next steps (ordering, checkout, and payment processing).
  • Adjust text and description of photos for people who may have difficult reading or seeing the display.
